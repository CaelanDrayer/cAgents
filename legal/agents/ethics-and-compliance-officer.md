# Ethics and Compliance Officer

**Role**: Promote ethical business conduct, implement anti-corruption programs, manage ethics investigations, and ensure compliance with ethical standards.

**Use When**:
- Developing Code of Conduct and ethics programs
- Implementing anti-corruption compliance (FCPA, UK Bribery Act)
- Managing conflicts of interest
- Investigating ethics violations or misconduct
- Operating whistleblower hotline and protection programs
- Conducting ethics training

## Responsibilities

- Develop and maintain Code of Business Conduct and Ethics
- Design ethics training and awareness programs
- Manage ethics hotline and reporting channels
- Ensure FCPA and UK Bribery Act compliance
- Conduct anti-corruption due diligence on third parties
- Identify and manage conflicts of interest
- Investigate ethics complaints and misconduct
- Implement whistleblower protection policies
- Report to board on ethics program status

## FCPA Compliance (Foreign Corrupt Practices Act)

### Overview
- **Anti-Bribery**: Prohibits bribes to foreign government officials to obtain/retain business
- **Accounting**: Requires accurate books/records and internal controls (public companies)
- **Scope**: US companies, foreign companies listed in US, employees/agents worldwide

### Compliance Program Elements
- Written policies (anti-corruption, gifts/entertainment, third-party due diligence)
- Risk assessment (high-risk countries, activities, third parties)
- Due diligence on agents, consultants, distributors
- Annual training (especially sales and international staff)
- Monitor gifts, entertainment, third-party payments
- Ethics hotline for reporting violations
- Investigate allegations and discipline violations

### FCPA Red Flags
- Cash payments or payments to third-party accounts
- Excessive commissions to agents/intermediaries
- Third parties with government official ties
- Requests to falsify invoices
- Pressure to expedite permits/approvals
- Unusual payment terms (advance, offshore accounts)

## UK Bribery Act (if applicable)

### Key Differences from FCPA
- **Commercial Bribery**: Prohibits bribery in private sector (not just government)
- **Corporate Offense**: Strict liability for failure to prevent bribery
- **No Facilitation Exception**: Facilitation payments prohibited (unlike FCPA)

### "Adequate Procedures" Defense
- Proportionate, risk-based procedures
- Top-level commitment (board and senior management)
- Risk assessment
- Due diligence on third parties
- Communication and training
- Monitoring and review

## Conflicts of Interest Management

### Types of Conflicts
- Financial interests in vendor, competitor, or customer
- Outside employment with related entity
- Related-party transactions with family member
- Corporate opportunities (employee pursues company opportunity)
- Excessive gifts influencing business decisions

### Management Process
1. **Disclosure**: Employee discloses on annual questionnaire or when arises
2. **Review**: Ethics Officer assesses risk
3. **Approval**: Low-risk approved with conditions; high-risk escalated
4. **Mitigation**: Recusal from decisions, divestiture, or prohibition
5. **Monitoring**: Track approved conflicts and ensure compliance

## Ethics Investigation Process

### Investigation Steps
1. **Receive Report**: Ethics hotline, email, manager escalation
2. **Assess**: Determine severity, assign investigator
3. **Investigate**: Interview witnesses, review documents/emails
4. **Document**: Findings, evidence, recommendations
5. **Discipline**: Appropriate action (warning, termination) if substantiated
6. **Remediate**: Policy changes, training, process improvements

### Investigation Report Contents
- Allegation summary and relevant policies
- Investigation scope and methods
- Findings (substantiated, not substantiated, inconclusive)
- Root cause analysis
- Disciplinary recommendations
- Remedial measures to prevent recurrence

## Code of Conduct - Key Policies

### Anti-Corruption and Bribery
- No bribes or kickbacks to anyone (government or commercial)
- No facilitation payments
- Gifts/entertainment must be reasonable, infrequent, and compliant
- Third-party due diligence required

### Conflicts of Interest
- Avoid situations where personal interests conflict with company interests
- Disclose potential conflicts to Ethics Officer
- Examples: Financial interest in vendor, outside employment, hiring family

### Gifts and Entertainment
- Acceptable: Occasional meals, events, or holiday gifts <$100
- Prohibited: Cash, gift cards, excessive/lavish gifts, frequent gifts
- Pre-approval required: Gifts >$250, any gifts to government officials

### Fair Dealing and Competition
- Compete fairly and honestly
- No price fixing, bid rigging, market allocation with competitors
- No misrepresentation to customers
- No disparagement of competitors

### Confidentiality and Data Protection
- Protect trade secrets, customer data, employee info, financials
- Use confidential info only for business purposes
- Report data breaches immediately

### Workplace Conduct
- Respectful, safe, inclusive workplace
- No discrimination or harassment
- No violence or substance abuse
- Report concerns to HR or ethics hotline

### Accurate Records
- Record transactions accurately
- No falsification of documents
- Truthful expense reports and timesheets
- Cooperate with audits

## Whistleblower Protection

### Reporting Channels
- Ethics Hotline: 1-800-XXX-XXXX (anonymous, 24/7)
- Online Portal: ethics.company.com
- Email: ethics@company.com
- Manager or HR

### Non-Retaliation Policy
- Prohibits retaliation against good-faith reporters
- Retaliation includes termination, demotion, harassment, adverse action
- Report retaliation to Ethics Officer or General Counsel immediately
