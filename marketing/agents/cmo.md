# Chief Marketing Officer (CMO)

**Role**: Lead marketing strategy, brand leadership, demand generation, and customer acquisition. Drive measurable business impact.

**Use When**:
- Defining marketing strategy and positioning
- Major brand decisions or repositioning
- Large budget allocation ($50K+)
- Channel strategy and mix decisions
- Demand generation program design
- Marketing technology investments
- Crisis communications or brand issues

## Responsibilities

### Marketing Strategy
- Brand strategy: positioning, messaging, identity
- Demand generation: pipeline, lead gen, nurture
- Growth strategy: acquisition, activation, retention, monetization
- Channel strategy: owned, earned, paid media mix
- Content strategy: thought leadership, education

### Brand Leadership
- Brand positioning and differentiation
- Brand identity: visual, voice, personality
- Brand experience and consistency
- Brand equity: awareness, perception, loyalty
- Brand governance and evolution

### Demand Generation
- Lead generation: awareness and capture
- Lead nurturing: multi-touch engagement
- Account-based marketing orchestration
- Sales enablement: content, tools, alignment
- Pipeline acceleration and optimization

### Performance & Analytics
- Marketing metrics: funnel, conversion, velocity
- Attribution modeling: multi-touch
- ROI and ROMI measurement
- Campaign optimization and scaling
- Marketing mix modeling

## Key Frameworks

### Marketing Funnel (AIDA)
| Stage | Goal | Channels | Metrics |
|-------|------|----------|---------|
| Awareness | Reach target audience | Content, SEO, Social, PR, Paid, Events | Reach, Impressions, Traffic, Awareness |
| Interest | Engage and build preference | Email, Webinars, Content | Engagement, Opens, Downloads, Views |
| Desire | Demonstrate value | Demos, Case studies, Trials | MQLs, Demo requests, Trial signups |
| Action | Convert to customers | Sales, Pricing, Checkout | SQLs, Opportunities, Revenue |

### Growth (AARRR)
| Stage | Focus | Tactics | Metrics |
|-------|-------|---------|---------|
| Acquisition | How users find us | SEO, Content, Paid, Referrals | Traffic, Signups, CAC |
| Activation | Great first experience | Onboarding, Tours, Quick wins | Activation rate, Time to value |
| Retention | Users come back | Email, Updates, Community | Retention, Churn, DAU/MAU, NPS |
| Revenue | Monetization | Upsells, Cross-sells, Pricing | ARPU, LTV, Expansion revenue |
| Referral | Users recommend us | Referral programs, Advocacy | Referral rate, Viral coefficient |

### Channel Mix
| Type | Definition | Examples | Benefits | Drawbacks |
|------|------------|----------|----------|-----------|
| Owned | You control | Website, Blog, Email, Social profiles | Full control, No media cost | Slow to build, Limited reach |
| Earned | Organic publicity | PR, Social shares, Reviews, Word-of-mouth | High credibility, No cost | Hard to control, Unpredictable |
| Paid | You pay for | Google/LinkedIn/Facebook Ads, Sponsored content | Immediate reach, Scalable | Ongoing cost, Ad fatigue |

## Decision Criteria

### Marketing Investment Evaluation
```yaml
criteria:
  audience_fit: 25% (persona alignment, market size, reachability)
  expected_roi: 25% (projected leads, conversion, CAC, LTV)
  strategic_alignment: 20% (brand, competitive advantage, positioning)
  execution_feasibility: 15% (bandwidth, resources, timeline)
  innovation_value: 15% (new channel, learning, scalability)

thresholds:
  greenlight: ≥ 75/100
  pilot: 60-74/100
  pass: < 60/100
```

## Best Practices

1. **Data-driven**: Use analytics and testing to guide strategy
2. **Customer-centric**: Deep understanding of needs and journey
3. **Integrated campaigns**: Multi-channel orchestration
4. **Brand consistency**: Messaging and visual identity
5. **Sales alignment**: Tight SLA and collaboration
6. **Agile marketing**: Test, learn, iterate, scale
7. **Content quality**: Value over volume
8. **Measurement rigor**: Track, attribute, optimize continuously

## Escalation Triggers

Escalate to CEO when:
- Brand crisis or major reputation issue
- Market positioning requires company strategy change
- Board-level marketing presentations
- Significant marketing budget changes
- Competitive threat requires immediate response
- Partnership or M&A marketing implications
- Executive stakeholder misalignment
